
## Comprehensive German Study Pathway

---

## A1 - Beginner Level

- Alphabet and pronunciation rules
    
- Basic greetings and introductions
    
- Personal pronouns and the verb sein﻿
    
- Definite and indefinite articles (der, die, das﻿ / ein, eine﻿)
    
- Present tense regular verbs conjugation
    
- Common irregular verbs (haben﻿, gehen﻿)
    
- Numbers 1-100
    
- Days of the week, months, dates
    
- Family members vocabulary
    
- Simple sentence structure: statements and questions
    
- Basic negation with nicht﻿ and kein﻿
    
- Modal verbs: können﻿, müssen﻿
    
- Simple prepositions of place (in﻿, auf﻿, unter﻿)
    
- Basic adjectives and their endings
    
- Asking and answering about time and weather
    
- Common everyday vocabulary: colors, food, clothes
    

---

## A2 - Elementary Level

- Past tense: Perfekt (formation and usage)
    
- Modal verbs in past tense
    
- Separable and inseparable verbs
    
- Pronouns: possessive and reflexive
    
- Word order in main and subordinate clauses
    
- Prepositions with accusative and dative cases
    
- Comparative and superlative adjectives
    
- Talking about hobbies, shopping, directions
    
- Expressing preferences and opinions
    
- Asking for and giving advice
    
- Expressing frequency and duration
    
- Vocabulary: travel, health, housing
    

---

## B1 - Intermediate Level

- Imperfect (Präteritum) vs. Perfekt usage
    
- Subjunctive II (Konjunktiv II﻿) for polite requests and hypothetical situations
    
- Passive voice (present and past)
    
- Relative clauses and pronouns
    
- Konjunktiv I for indirect speech
    
- Advanced prepositions and conjunctions
    
- Expanded modal verb uses
    
- Expressing cause and consequence
    
- Vocabulary: work, education, feelings, relationships
    
- Describing experiences and events in detail
    
- Expressing opinions, doubts, and assumptions
    
- Writing informal and formal emails/letters
    

---

## B2 - Upper Intermediate Level

- Complex sentence structures with multiple subordinate clauses
    
- Advanced adjective endings and noun gender exceptions
    
- Nominalization and infinitive constructions
    
- Advanced subjunctive and passive forms
    
- Idiomatic expressions and phrasal verbs
    
- Expressing emphasis, contrast, and concession
    
- Vocabulary for politics, society, culture, and media
    
- Debating and discussing abstract topics
    
- Writing formal reports and essays
    
- Understanding and analyzing news and films
    
- Listening to and understanding native-speed conversations and broadcasts

## C1 - Advanced Level

- Mastery of subtle nuances in grammar, including advanced conjunctive structures
    
- Full range of passive forms, including causative and complex structures
    
- Extensive use of idiomatic expressions and cultural references
    
- Deep understanding of register shifts (formal vs. informal)
    
- Complex grammatical forms: mixed conditionals, advanced subjunctive for wishes and hypotheses
    
- Stylistic variations in written and spoken language
    
- Critical reading of literature, academic texts, and media
    
- Producing well-structured essays, reports, and presentations
    
- Extensive listening comprehension of fast, idiomatic speech and debates
    
- Active usage of a broad vocabulary, including specialized terminology in arts, sciences, and professional fields
